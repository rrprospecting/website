For Landowners Meet R&R Prospecting
Who We Are

Exploration rooted in family and the outdoors.

We hunt, camp, hike, study geology, and spend countless hours in mountain country. These places are not simply where we work. They are where our family makes memories.

R&R Prospecting grew from a genuine interest in understanding the land—its geology, mineral history, waterways, wildlife, and the people who care for it. Our long-term goal is to build a responsible small-scale exploration and mining operation without losing sight of why these landscapes matter.

The way we explore matters just as much as what we discover.
Our Commitment

Respect comes first.

We believe responsible mineral exploration begins with respect—for the land, the landowner, and future generations.

Every project is guided by professionalism, scientific integrity, common sense, protection of water resources, and reclamation to the highest standard. These principles define how we operate and the reputation we intend to earn.

Why Stewardship Matters

Reclamation is personal.

We do not view reclamation as a box to check or merely a requirement of law.

We spend our time in these mountains with our families. We camp here, hunt here, learn here, and enjoy the water and wildlife that make these places special. Protecting those values is part of every responsible exploration decision.

Our standard is straightforward: plan carefully, disturb only what is necessary, protect water, communicate openly, and reclaim disturbed ground to the highest standard.

Landowner Partnerships

A professional introduction for property owners.

If you own land in historic mining country or an area with geological potential, R&R Prospecting welcomes a respectful conversation.

01

Listen First

We begin with the landowner’s goals, concerns, property history, boundaries, access, and expectations.

02

Research Before Disturbance

Historic records, geological maps, ownership information, and field observations help determine whether further work is justified.

03

Clear Agreements

Permission, scope, access, communication, compensation, reclamation, and next steps should be understood before work begins.

04

Small, Measured Steps

Early evaluation should answer questions while limiting unnecessary disturbance and expense.

05

Protect Water and Property

Water resources, livestock, roads, fences, crops, timber, wildlife habitat, and privacy are treated with care.

06

Reclaim to the Highest Standard

Reclamation is planned as part of the work—not treated as an afterthought.

Research, education, and field experience.

Future articles and field reports will cover geological maps, faults, intrusions, placer and lode deposits, mineral identification, historic mining districts, prospecting equipment, sampling, safety, water protection, reclamation, and lessons learned in the field.
